Intrinsic vs Extrinsic Curiosity

Judith introduced an important distinction:

"Curiosity, per se, is complex. Intrinsic vs extrinsicâ€¦situation, knowledge, experiences,. Perhaps worth addl in depth discussion."

This framework suggests that curiosity arises from both:

  • Intrinsic sources - internal drives and interests
  • Extrinsic sources - external situations, stimuli, and contexts

Mother's Teaching Method

Judith shared a powerful personal story about how her mother cultivated curiosity:

"As a child, whenever I asked a question, my mom would reply 'what do you think?', which led to expansive discussion."

This approach:

  • Reflects the question back to the child
  • Values the child's thinking
  • Creates space for expansive discussion rather than simple answers
  • Builds metacognitive skills
