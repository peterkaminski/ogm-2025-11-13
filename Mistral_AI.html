Mistral AI

Note: This topic was mentioned during the call but not discussed in depth.

Victoria (Spain) recommended Mistral AI as an alternative to US-dominated AI models, offering more European perspectives.

Why Mistral Matters

Victoria observed:

"AI always ignores us, Europeans, the middle ground ðŸ˜­"

"I can talk from experience, I always have to remind the AI to consider European options in their answers"

Her recommendation:

"@Pete Kaminski Mistral AI"

The European Perspective Problem

Victoria identified that mainstream AI models (like ChatGPT, Claude) have US-centric biases:

  • Training data dominated by US sources
  • Default assumptions reflect American culture
  • European contexts get ignored or relegated to "other"
  • Need to explicitly prompt for European perspectives

Mistral as Alternative

Mistral AI is a French AI company offering:

  • European-developed models
  • More European content in training
  • Different cultural assumptions
  • Alternative to US tech dominance

Broader Issue

Pete Kaminski acknowledged:

"It wouldn't be easy, but it could be done to collect European-centric information to train models. Europe does have a wealth of information going back centuries that would be super valuable."

Challenges:

  • Much European content not digitized
  • Language diversity
  • Different data privacy laws (GDPR)
  • Resource requirements

Connection to Curiosity

This relates to Cultural Dimensions of Curiosity:

  • If AI shapes how we ask and answer questions
  • And AI has cultural biases
  • Then it affects what curiosity paths seem available
  • Access to diverse AI models enables more curiosity

The Training Data Question

"What we need is a non-US dominated internet for our training models ðŸ˜‚"

This raises questions about:

  • Digital colonialism
  • Whose knowledge gets preserved and amplified
  • Cultural diversity in AI
  • Right to see your own culture reflected
